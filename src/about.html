What is a WotUS?

A Waterway of the US, a.k.a. WotUS, is essentially a body of water that is protected by Environmental Protection Agency rules and regulations. A WotUS can be a lake, river, stream, or even a dry waterbed. The goal of the inital laws put in place was to protect from polution any waterways that are involved in commerce or are drinking water sources. The goal of this project is to provide a definition for a WotUS that is objective and based on reproducible math. Ideally, our definition would be used as a guideline to help inpsectors of waterways decide on one value to define a WotUS. The definition we have created is based on the amount of water that flows through a point in the waterway annually. Any point on the waterway that has an amount of water flowing through it each year that is above a chosen threshold is considered a Waterway of the US.

What is a senior project?

At Southern Illinois University Edwardsville, SIUE, the big finish for a computer science major is the two semester sequence known as senior project. A senior project is a glimpse into what work will be like once we graduate. We work on a real project for a real client that could be used well into the future. Some clients are professors on campus while others are people from industry. There is a wide variety of projects ranging from our project to training simulators to intricate websites and beyond. We also learn about agile progamming hands on during senior project. Teams work in two week sprints and maintain a backlog and burndown chart to track progress and work remaining.
